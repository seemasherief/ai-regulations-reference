# ai-regulations-reference
I built this because AI without guardrails is dangerous. A structured reference covering EU AI Act, GDPR, CCPA and SB 942. These filtered to what actually applies to Customer Service AI systems, with fines and CS scenarios for each article.

**[View the live tool here](https://seemasherief.github.io/ai-regulations-reference/)**

## Purpose & Scope

An interactive single-page reference covering **22 articles and sections** across four frameworks, each one translated into a customer service context with the relevant fine structures included.

No legal jargon without explanation. No abstract compliance checklists. Every article comes with a real-world CS scenario so you can see exactly where the risk lives.

**Frameworks covered:**

| Framework | Scope | Key penalty |
|---|---|---|
| EU AI Act | World's first comprehensive AI regulation; risk-based tiers | Up to €35M or 7% global turnover |
| GDPR | Any organisation processing EU/EEA resident data | Up to €20M or 4% global turnover |
| CCPA / CPRA | California residents' privacy rights | Up to $7,500 per intentional violation |
| California SB 942 | AI transparency and disclosure; in effect Jan 2026 | $5,000 per violation per day |

## What's inside

**Overview tab** - stats at a glance (22 articles, 9 critical severity, 11 high severity), full-text search across all articles and the four real scenarios this reference was built to prevent.

**Framework tabs** - EU AI Act, GDPR, CCPA, SB 942, each with fine structures and a clickable article list. Click any row to open a detailed modal with:

- The regulatory text, summarised plainly
- A concrete CS scenario (the situation where this bites you)
- Why it matters at operational scale
- The exact fine structure from primary sources

**Cross-framework tab** - a side-by-side comparison of which obligations appear across frameworks, so you can identify overlap and prioritise.

**Why this matters tab** - the context for why CS operations specifically are in the regulatory crosshairs right now, and what changed with the arrival of AI in the contact centre.

---

## How to use it

Download the HTML file and open it in any browser - no server, no dependencies, no install. Everything runs locally.

Or view it directly via GitHub Pages.

Use the **search bar on the overview tab** to find any article by keyword, regulation name, topic, scenario type or fine amount.

## A note on accuracy

Every fine figure is verified from primary regulatory sources. Articles are cited by their official reference (e.g. *Article 22 GDPR*, *Section 1798.120 CCPA*, *Article 50 EU AI Act*). This is a reference tool, not legal advice. For compliance decisions, consult qualified counsel.
